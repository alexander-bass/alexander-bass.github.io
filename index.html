Hello! Earlier today I was working on implementing cel shading into a program I am writing for CSC155, so I will write about that! To do this, I first had to figure out how a cel shader differs from my current Blinn-Phong lighting implementation. The main differences lie in how diffuse and specular lighting characteristics are calculated. Blinn-Phong calculates diffuse lighting based on the angle between the light and the surface normals of an object and produces a smooth, continuous gradient from light to shadow. Cel shading differs by breaking this smooth transition in to distanct bands of color, which gives it a more cartoony, hand-drawn look. Very cool! Similarly, specular highlights are simplified in cel shading, from a smooth gradient to a single sharp white spot. I also had to figure out how to do edge detection, but this is long enough. Bye!
